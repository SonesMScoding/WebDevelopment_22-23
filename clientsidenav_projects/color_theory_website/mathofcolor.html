Trichromatic Theory

The human retina has 3 cones that absorb color. The trichromatic theory in it's simplest form views color space as three dimensional.

It is believed that colors on the color wheel can be measured by how much of each cone in our eye converts light into color. Colors can be measured by their red, green, and blue values. (RGB)
