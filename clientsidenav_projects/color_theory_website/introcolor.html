Where it All Starts: The Color Wheel

The color wheel is made up of three primary colors, three secondary colors, and six tertiary colors.


Primary Color - Red, blue, yellow. These are the base of the color wheel. All other colors can be made using these colors.(other than magenta, I will make a page on magenta one day)

Secondary Color - Created by mixing two primary colors, there are three colors that can be produces. Purple, green, and orange are the secondary colors.

Tertiary Color - Each of these six colors, are combinations of primary colors and secondary colors. Red-orange, red-purple, yellow-orange, yellow-green, blue-green, and blue-purple are the in between colors in the color wheel.


Designers and artists get deep into the color wheel and really mess it up to create all the diverse visuals we have today.

Color theory reminds creatives that there are some combinations and practices that assist in consistently pleasing aesthetics.
